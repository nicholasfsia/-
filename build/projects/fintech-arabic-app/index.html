Fintech Arabic Mobile App

A B2B Android Arabic mobile app that facilitates microinsurance agents selling microinsurance in Upper Egypt. COMPANY

FairVentures


ROLE

UX Designer, one of the two designers at FairVentures


TOOLS

GV Sprint, Figma, InVision


TIMELINE

6 weeks


TL;DR

Microinsurance is a solution for low income families to have a financial security. However, with the sparsely distributed living location of most low income families, distribution of insurance becomes a problem. An insurance company in Egypt needed help from us to solve microinsurance distribution problem in the Upper Egypt. The idea of microinsurance is also still foreign for the people who live in Upper Egypt. A Google Venture design sprint was done to solve these problems. In addition, a research and implementation on Arabic localization was also done by me. The sprint resulted in three successful main features, including product information, story, and client profile. The project resulted in positive feedbacks from the user testing participants and stakeholders; and was handed off for phase 2 to the stakeholders, which initial MVP and trial with limited microinsurance agents are expected.

Problems

In big cities of third world countries, insurance is already considered a common thing. However, the idea and concept of insurance in the suburbs of third world countries are still novel, yet they probably need it more than people who have extensive access to it. The big problem with insurance is it tends to be expensive and is not an easy concept to understand. Microinsurance is a solution to this high price problem. Microinsurance is insurance products that offer coverage to low-income households. However, there is a distribution problem of microinsurance, especially in Upper Egypt. Microinsurance in Upper Egypt is quite hard to find and not easily accessible.

Goals

Ultimate goals

Build, sustain and nurture trusted insurance agent to offer insurance protection to their network of family, friends and clients. Also, to increase risk protection for low to middle income segments in Upper Egypt.

Micro goal

“How might we digitally enable insurance agents to identify needs and distribute defined service(s) to their prospective clients?”

Approach

GV Design Sprint

A Google Venture design sprint approach was done to brainstorm solutions to the problem. To narrow down the scope of the project we focused on a specific persona and event. We chose an insurance agent as our target persona and selling as the target event. The insurance agent incentives are to obtain commissions, community involvement and support, and grow client base. They have also sold microloans, similar concept to microinsurance but for loans, thus they already have existing client base and know how to interact with the potential clients.

Arabization (Arabic localization)

In addition to the GV design sprint, I did research on Arabic localization. My approach was to research on Arabic language and how it is used in the app context. How to effectively and efficiently design for Arabic language? Below I’m going to explain some points that I think are valuable to share as a reference for other projects.

Reading pattern and cultural context

Arabic readers reading pattern on Arabic sites mirrors the F-shaped reading pattern. When choosing images, it’s crucial to not fall into using common stereotypes (attention to detail, choose images specific to the targeted region).

F-reading pattern of this app highlighted yellow

Bidirectionality

UIs for languages that are read from right-to-left (RTL), such as Arabic, should be mirrored to ensure content is easy to understand. LTR to RTL (or vice-versa), is often called mirroring. Not all components are mirrored and that is because of the pattern they have developed.


When a UI changes from one direction to another, these items are not mirrored:

  • Icons that do not communicate direction, such as camera
  • Numbers, such as the clock and phone numbers
  • Untranslated text (even if it’s part of a phrase)
  • Charts and graphs

When a UI is mirrored, these changes occur:

  • Text fields icons are displayed on the opposite side of a field
  • Navigation buttons are displayed in reverse order
  • Icons that communicate direction, like arrows, are mirrored
  • Text (if it is translated to an RTL language) is aligned to the right
The navigation bar language comparison

For example, on the navigation bar above, the navigation order is mirrored. Some icons, such as Stories and Products icons are mirrored. Even though either icon doesn't communicate direction explicitly, it does hold a direction. For Stories icon, if you open iMessage or WhatsApp in Arabic, the chat “balloon” is mirrored due to reading pattern/direction. For Products icon, the icon resembles a list with bullet points; and in Arabic, list and bullet points are also mirrored due to Arabic reading pattern/direction. The Clients and More icons don’t need to be mirrored as they don’t communicate direction. But what about Tracking icon? In my opinion, it shouldn’t be mirrored. Yes, the icon communicates direction, but it communicates a graph or trend direction. Graphs are not mirrored even in Arabic thus the icon does not need to be mirrored.

Final Design

Product line

The product line feature is the agent’s source of truth to obtain details on products available. This feature lists all the product lines that the agents can sell with description and all the necessary information. For the main list view, I consulted with the stakeholders on what is the most important information for the agent to sell. The top three main information is coverage limit, price, and insurance name. Then I designed the list so that agents can get that information quickly without going to the product details.

The product screens

Stories

The stories feature provides agents materials to utilize empathy to educate clients about microinsurance. By using story, it helps the potential customers to understand the concept of microinsurance. The story piece is not just to let the potential customers empathize, but also to educate the potential customers about microinsurance and why it’s important for them, how realistically can microinsurance help people when something unexpected happens.

The stories screens

Client profile

Client profile is the place where agents can obtain or write information about the clients. The client profile works similarly as contact profile commonly found in any mobile OS. It holds client’s phone number and address, also, there are fields for client’s personal detail like family members, religion, and client’s microinsurance related information. This will benefit the agents by allowing them to record and recall client context information. The client profile is necessary to separate work and personal, also provide details specific to the app context.

The profile screens

User Testing and Validation

Even though we didn’t conduct the user testing ourselves, we developed guidelines and scripts for interview testing with the insurance agents in the Upper Egypt with the stakeholders. All the recordings were sent back from Egypt, then we translated and analyzed the results. From this experience, we gained some validations, feedbacks, and learning. The story feature is very desirable as it helps the customer to empathize with other people. The product information and client profile features are really helpful on the insurance agent end. It helps them to keep track on their client and the products available. Overall, the prototype was successful and received positively by the agents.


In addition, through the user testing experience, we learnt some dos and don’ts of user interview and testing in Egypt.


Do's

  • Ask the participant whether they’re comfortable to be recorded or not
  • Expect some women are not comfortable to be recorded (cultural)
  • Have an outside translator to translate recording to reduce biases (he/she tends to translate word per word as the translator has little knowledge about the project)

Dont's

  • Doing user interview and testing during Ramadhan may slow down your ETA
  • Overcrowding the room with people, especially with men when interviewing a woman can create an uncomfortable environment

Learning and Next Step

From our testing, participants (guardians) agreed that stories had played a big role in their career and they found that this story feature is really valuable. Client profile is crucial for guardians to recall information about the clients. In addition, the kind of information it contains should be explored more through questionnaires or interview with the microinsurance agents.
